# Asset type: Agency website

An agency-owned public site. The most exposed surface in the channel: publicly accessible, rarely pre-approved, and the first place out-of-compliance material gets found. Agencies are run by entrepreneurs; they build their own materials, and technically all of it requires home-office approval before use. The website is where that rule gets skipped.

## Where agency sites fail first

1. Brand identity misuse: the site presents as the carrier instead of the agency's own name (A13). This is the single most destructive agency-site violation.
2. Unsourced figures: growth percentages, production rankings, income numbers with no asterisk and no source (A5, A6).
3. The full recruiting-rule breakage in one place: provide language, bare income claims, invented titles (A1, A5, A8).
4. Stale or unverifiable institutional claims: ratings without year, rank claims that no longer hold (A6, A12).

## Review emphasis, in order

1. Branding first: whose name is on the masthead, the copyright line, the contact block. Carrier-presenting agency sites are blocked pending approval (A13).
2. Every figure on every page: asterisk, same-page source, completed year.
3. Titles across leadership pages against the profile ladder; corporate-ladder titles layered over Agent titles are title-washing (A8).
4. Term sweep per `reference/term-banks.md`.
5. Imagery licensing and brand-values check (A14).
6. Publicly checkable claims verified and reported (A12).

## Asset notes

- Same-page means same page: a source on the About page does not cover a claim on the Careers page.
- For a live site found out of compliance, the approval and correction workflow in the company profile applies: notify the agency, offer the no-charge review, put the change list on the record. The documented review is the due-diligence record.
