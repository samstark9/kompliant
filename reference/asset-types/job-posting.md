# Asset type: Job posting

Postings on hiring platforms and professional networks. This is where title-washing lives, because postings are the least-reviewed surface in the channel (B6): a softened title might "work" in a posting precisely because postings evade review. The rules are identical anyway.

## Where postings fail first

1. Disguise titles: benefits representative, enrollment specialist, anything that removes "sales" from the role (A8). "Sales rep" is acceptable; erasing the sales function is not.
2. Title-washing in the management pitch: Director, Senior Partner, President in place of Agent-bearing titles (A8).
3. Employment vocabulary forced by the platform's framing: job, hire, salary, benefits fields (A2, A3, A11). The platform calling its listings "jobs" does not license the copy to.
4. Compressed advancement promises in the hook: "management in 90 days" (A7).
5. Part-time targeting with nothing behind it (B2).

## Review emphasis, in order

1. Posting title and every title in the body against the profile ladder.
2. Term sweep per `reference/term-banks.md`, with extra attention to platform-shaped vocabulary.
3. Compensation section: hedged forms only, asterisk convention; platform compensation fields set to commission, never a salary range.
4. Timeline and advancement claims.
5. The channel-risk note (B6) in the review when a violation looks posting-specific.

## Asset notes

- Postings rarely carry small type. A posting that states figures must still carry the proof trail within the posting body; "the details are on our site" does not satisfy same-page.
- The employer-name field follows A13: the agency posts as itself, never as the carrier.
