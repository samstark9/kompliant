# Intake interview

The scripted "onboard a new company" interview. Run it when the operator says "onboard a new company," "set up a profile," or attempts a review with no completed profile.

Procedure: ask in order, one section at a time. Write answers into a copy of `reference/company-profile.template.md`. Read the completed draft back for confirmation, then save it as `reference/company-profile.md`. Do not invent answers. Do not skip the income table; without it the False Claims family cannot be checked.

## Script

Open with: "Before this desk reviews anything, I need the company on file. This takes about ten minutes. Your answers become the standard every draft is checked against."

### Identity
1. Full legal name of the carrier, and the short brand form used in copy.
2. Founding year.
3. Confirm the field model: is every field agent a 1099 independent contractor with the home office, at every level? (If no, stop: this desk only reviews commission-only 1099 contractor recruiting copy.)
4. Sales model: virtual, in-person appointment, door-to-door, or mixed? What should copy reflect?
5. Lead model: where do recruiting and sales leads come from, and what may copy say about them? Any lead-magnet items? (Remember: leads are "made available," never "provided.")

### Ladder
6. Every level, bottom to top, exact titles as they must appear on materials. Confirm every management title carries the word Agent.
7. Any accepted informal aliases (for conversation only), and any titles explicitly banned.

### Income table
8. For each level: average income for the most recently completed year, and average time to achieve. Named data source and year.
9. Top-earner figures, if the company lists them.
10. The company's required small-type wording, if it has one; otherwise the reference form in `reference/disclosure-mechanics.md` applies.

### Public facts
11. Every publicly checkable claim copy is allowed to make: Fortune rank (exact), ratings (rating, title, year), years in business, claims paid, in-force amount, stock listing. Exact values; these become the verification baseline.

### Brand
12. Branding rules for agency materials: whose name appears, what approval is required, what carrier assets agencies may use.
13. The approval and correction workflow: who reviews, at what cost, who owns compliance, what escalation looks like.

### Close
14. Anything state-specific the desk should flag on sight (separate-entity states, state filing rules)?

Read back the completed profile. On confirmation, save and state: "Profile on file. The desk is open for review."
