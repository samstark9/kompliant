# Examples

Worked examples of the rulebook applied. Rules live in `rules.md`; this file only shows them in action. All company names and figures are from the fabricated demo profile in `reference/company-profile.md`.

## 1. The canonical income table

This is the gold-standard correction in this domain. Study the difference; it carries all three fatal-error families.

WRONG:

> Career Level: Annual Income
> Principal Agent: $900k+, 3+ years
> District Agent: $450k, 3 months to 3 years
> Associate Agent: $90k, 1 to 90 days

RIGHT:

> Career Level: Average Income*, Average Time to Achieve*
> Principal Agent: $900k+, 6+ years
> District Agent: $210k, 3+ years
> Associate Agent: $45k, n/a
>
> Small type: *Stated averages are per 2025 end of year internal sales reporting, Ambervale Life Insurance Company. Actual income and time to achieve are based on individual sales and recruiting performance, and are not guaranteed.

What changed and why:

- "Annual Income" became "Average Income*". A bare figure is a promise. An average with an asterisk is a fact with a proof trail (A5, A6).
- $450k became $210k. The old figure was the top slice presented as typical: a False Claim.
- $90k in 1 to 90 days became $45k, n/a. The old cell was an Empty Perceptional Promise, roughly double the real early average with a timeline attached.
- Timelines stretched to the real averages and joined the asterisk convention (A7).
- The small type appeared: same page, no smaller than 8pt, most recently completed year, named data source, "not guaranteed" (A6, mechanics in `reference/disclosure-mechanics.md`).

## 2. Annotated critiques

### 2a. The provide-and-free opener

Draft line:

> "We provide industry-leading training and free leads to every new hire."

Critique:

- A1 BLOCK. "We provide." You cannot say we provide anything; provide implies required, required implies employment. The compliant forms are the available forms: training available, leads made available.
- A10 BLOCK. "Free." Always "no cost."
- A3 BLOCK. "Hire." Internal term only. Never in external materials.
- One sentence, three blocks, and every one of them lands in the Employment Implication family. This sentence cannot ship in any form close to its current one. The wording is yours to fix.

### 2b. The timeline promise

Draft line:

> "Management in 90 days. Your path to $450,000 starts now."

Critique:

- A7 BLOCK. "Management in 90 days" is an achievement-time claim stated as fact. If it does not happen, it is the basis of a claim against the principal. The only acceptable shape is "typically X to Y months," asterisked to the average.
- A5 and A6 BLOCK. $450,000 is bare: no hedge, no asterisk, no source. Against the demo profile it is also the top slice of District Agent income presented as typical, which makes it a False Claim, and paired with the 90-day line it reads as a promise, which makes it an Empty Perceptional Promise. All three families in nine words.

### 2c. The gray flag

Draft line:

> "Do work you really love."

Critique:

- B1 FLAG. "Work" here is not framing a job; it is framing an activity. It can pass. But it sits one edit away from "full-time work" or "steady work," which cannot. Flagged with reasoning; the writer decides.
